At its monetary policy meeting in December 2013, the Central Bank of the Dominican Republic decided to maintain the monetary policy rate at 6.25% per annum unchanged.

The decision regarding the leading rate takes into account inflation projections, as well as market expectations. The interannual inflation rate decreased for the third consecutive month, from its highest level of the year of 5.67% in the month of July, to 4.14% in November. Accumulated inflation to the eleventh month of 2013 was 3.49%, given the monthly variation of just 0.06%. Likewise, the interannual core inflation was reduced to 4.45%.

In the external context, the outlook for the world economy has improved, driven by the process of gradual recovery exhibited by the industrialized economies. Emerging economies continue to grow, although they show a slight deceleration with respect to the levels reached in recent years. The growth of the United States of America was revised upwards, standing at 2.0% in year-on-year terms and 4.1% in annualized quarterly terms. This growth has contributed to reduce unemployment to 7.0%, a decrease of more than one percentage point so far this year. On the other hand, macroeconomic indicators point to Europe coming out of recession. Inflationary pressures remain low in line with the behavior of commodity prices during the current year.

In the domestic sphere, economic activity and domestic demand show a dynamism of economic growth above 3.0%, projected in the macroeconomic framework for 2013. Credit to the private sector in national currency would be closing the year with a year-on-year growth greater than 13.5%, while improving prospects for activities related to foreign trade, mainly tourism, free zones and gold exports.

The Central Bank reaffirms its commitment to conduct monetary policy to achieve its inflation target, while continuing to monitor the evolution of the world economy and the domestic situation, in order to take the necessary measures against possible risks to the stability of the economy. prices and the proper functioning of the financial and payment systems.
